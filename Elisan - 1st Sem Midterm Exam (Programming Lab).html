Mercury

Diameter: 4,879.4 km

Mass: 3.30 x 10^23 kg

Average Temperature: 167°C

Moons: None

Orbital Period: days

The smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon. From the surface of Mercury, the Sun would appear more than three times as large as it does when viewed from Earth, and the sunlight would be as much as seven times brighter. Mercury's surface resembles that of Earth's moon, scarred by many impact craters resulting from collisions with meteoroids and comets.

Venus

Diameter: 12,104 km

Mass: 4.86 x 10^24 kg

Average Temperature: 460°C

Moons: None

Orbital Period: days

Similar in size and structure to Earth, Venus has been called Earth's twin. Venus and Earth are similar in size, mass, density, composition, and gravity. However, Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide and it's perpetually shrouded in thick, yellowish clouds of mostly sulfuric acid that trap heat, causing a runaway greenhouse effect. Despite Mercury being closer to the Sun, Venus is the hottest planet in the entire solar system.

Earth

Diameter: 12,742 km

Mass: 5.97 x 10^24 kg

Average Temperature: 15°C

Moons: 1 (The Moon)

Orbital Period: days

Earth—our home planet—is the only place we know of so far that's inhabited by living things. Earth's atmosphere is 78 percent nitrogen, 21 percent oxygen and 1 percent other ingredients—the perfect balance to breathe and live.It's also the only planet in our solar system with liquid water on the surface. Earth is the biggest terrestrial planet, all of which are made of rock and metal, and the only planet with a single moon.

Mars

Diameter: 6,779 km

Mass: 6.41 x 10^23 kg

Average Temperature: -60°C

Moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)

Orbital Period: days

Mars is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere. Named by the ancient Romans for their god of war because its reddish color was reminiscent of blood, Mars' reddish look is due to oxidization—or rusting—of iron in the rocks, regolith (Martian “soil”), and dust on it's surface that gets kicked up into the atmosphere that from a distance, makes the planet appear mostly red.

Jupiter

Diameter: 139,820 km

Mass: 1.89 x 10^27 kg

Average Temperature: -145°C

Moons: 95 (Jupiter's four largest moons, called the Galilean satellites after Italian Astronomer Galileo Galilei, are Europa, Ganymede, Callisto, and Io)

Orbital Period: days

Jupiter is, by far, the largest planet in the solar system - more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined. It is a giant planet composed mainly of hydrgen and helium; as a gas giant, Jupiter has no solid surface. Jupiter is well-known for The Great Red Spot, a storm that has been raging on for 100 years.

Saturn

Diameter: 116,460 km

Mass: 5.68 x 10^26 kg

Average Temperature: -288.4°C

Moons: 146 (Seven Major Moons: Mimas, Enceladus, Tethys, Dione, Rhea, Titan, and Iapetus)

Orbital Period: days

Adorned with thousands of beautiful ringlets, Saturn is unique among the planets. It is not the only planet to have rings—made of chunks of ice and rock—but none are as spectacular or as complicated as Saturn's. Saturn's rings are thought to be pieces of comets, asteroids or shattered moons that broke up before they reached the planet, torn apart by Saturn's powerful gravity, and orbiting around the planet at different speeds.

Uranus

Diameter: 50,724 km

Mass: 8.68 x 10^25 kg

Average Temperature: -195°C

Moons: 27 (Five Major Moons: Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon)

Orbital Period: days

Uranus is very cold and windy. It is surrounded by faint rings and more than two dozen small moons as it rotates at a nearly 90-degree angle from the plane of its orbit. This unique tilt makes Uranus appear to spin on its side. Uranus is blue-green in color due to large amounts of methane, which absorbs red light but allows blues to be reflected back into space. The atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, but also includes large amounts of water, ammonia and methane. The planet was named after Uranus, the Greek god of the sky, who was also the father of Kronos (or Saturn in Roman mythology).

Neptune

Diameter: 49,244 km

Mass: 1.02 x 10^26 kg

Average Temperature: -214°C

Moons: 14 (Major Moon: Triton)

Orbital Period: days

Dark, cold and whipped by supersonic winds, giant Neptune is the eighth and most distant major planet orbiting our Sun. More than 30 times as far from the Sun as Earth, Neptune is not visible to the naked eye. In 2011, Neptune completed its first 165-year orbit since its discovery. The planet's rich blue color comes from methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red wavelengths of light but allows blue ones to be reflected back into space.
